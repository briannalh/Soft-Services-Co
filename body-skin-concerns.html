Some of the most common concerns that we see affect body skin are:
Keratosis Pilaris (KP)
Keratosis pilaris (KP), also referred to as “chicken skin” and “permanent goosebumps,” is reported to affect 40% of adult Americans. These small pesky bumps, commonly found on back of arms, thighs, calves are caused by a build-up of keratin on our skin which plugs and clogs the hair follicle.


addressed by: Buffing Bar, Smoothing Solution, Carea Cream
Ingrown Hair
Ingrown hair is a trapped hair that's curled back into the skin, leading our skin to identify it as an invading foreign body. This causes inflammation in the area, such as a red or brown cyst that can feel painful to the touch. Gentle and consistent exfoliation in between shaving is the best course of action.


addressed by: Buffing Bar, Smoothing Solution
Strawberry Skin
“Strawberry skin” refers to the shaving-damaged follicles, commonly on legs, that appear enlarged and clogged, creating the appearance of small dark spots resembling strawberry seeds.


addressed by: Smoothing Solution
Discoloration
Discoloration is a general term that indicates an area of skin, such as a dot or a patch, that's darker or lighter than someone’s base tone. It encompasses different kinds of pigmentation concerns such as hyperpigmentation (from acne or burns, whether from the sun or in the kitchen), hypopigmentation, melasma, tinea versicolor, vitiligo, etc.

Dry, Dehydrated,
Dull Skin
Dry skin is a skin type lacks lipids ands loses more moisture than it's able to maintain; itchy and flakey skin are common signs. Dehydrated/dull skin is a skin condition that can affect all skin types (even those of us who are oil slicks), and signifies a lack of hydration. This concern can lead to dry skin, and presents itself similarly, in addition to signs like: tight feeling skin, a lack of plumpness and bounciness, and lacking overall brightness.
